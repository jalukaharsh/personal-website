# Building a Personal Website in 2024

Every developer should have a personal website. It's your digital home, portfolio, and blog all in one place. Here's how to build one that stands out.

## Planning Your Site

Before writing any code, consider what you want to achieve:

- **Showcase your work** - Display projects and accomplishments
- **Share your knowledge** - Write blog posts and tutorials
- **Build your brand** - Establish your online presence
- **Connect with others** - Provide ways for people to reach you

## Choosing Your Tech Stack

The modern web offers many excellent options. Here are some popular choices:

### Static Site Generators

- **Astro** - Best for content-heavy sites
- **Next.js** - Great for React developers
- **Hugo** - Extremely fast builds
- **Eleventy** - Simple and flexible

### Styling Options

1. Tailwind CSS - Utility-first approach
2. CSS Modules - Scoped styling
3. Styled Components - CSS-in-JS
4. Plain CSS - Sometimes simple is best

## Content Strategy

Your website needs great content. Here's what to include:

```markdown
- About page - Tell your story
- Projects - Showcase your best work
- Blog - Share your expertise
- Contact - Make it easy to reach you
```

## Deployment

Modern hosting platforms make deployment trivial:

- **Netlify** - Automatic deployments from Git
- **Vercel** - Optimized for Next.js
- **GitHub Pages** - Free hosting for static sites
- **Cloudflare Pages** - Fast global CDN

## Tips for Success

> "Your website is never finished. It's a living document that grows with you."

Keep these principles in mind:

- Start simple and iterate
- Focus on content over complexity
- Make it mobile-friendly
- Optimize for performance
- Update regularly

## Conclusion

Building a personal website is an investment in yourself. Take your time, make it yours, and don't be afraid to experiment. The web is your canvas!
